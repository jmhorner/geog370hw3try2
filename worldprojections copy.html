In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

I was able to display the same map in different projections, by clicking the projection tool in the bottom righhand corner of the QGIS application. From there, I exported the different projections as images using the layout tool.

WGS84 Projection

This appears to be concerned with preserving the shape of landmasses.

Aitoff Projection

This projection appears to try and more accurately project the eliptical shape of the Earth onto a flat surface.

Psuedo Mercator Projection

This preserves the shape of landmasses.

Sphere_Winkel_I Projection

This attempts to preserve the spherical form of the Earth.

World_Cylindrical_Equal_Area Projection

This projection appears to be concerned with preserving the real life areas of the countries.

World Equidistant Conic Projection

This is a nice top down view.

North Pole Azimuthal Projection

The further south of the North Pole, the more distorted the map is.

Oregon Columbia River West Zone Projection

In preserving a specific part of Oregon, most of the world is severely distorted.

Africa Sinusoidal Projection

The central focus of this projection is the continent of Africa
